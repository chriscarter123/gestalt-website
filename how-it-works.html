The Technology

Point. Recognize. Listen.

Gestalt uses computer vision to identify artwork the moment you raise your phone. No QR codes to hunt for. No beacon hardware to install. No app store scavenger hunt. Just a camera pointed at art, and a voice that tells you everything you're looking at.

From camera to context in under a second

1

You point

Open Gestalt and aim your phone at any painting, sculpture, or mural. The app doesn't need perfect framing — partial views, angles, even reflections work.

2

We recognize

Our neural network compares what you see against a database of indexed artworks. Identification happens in milliseconds, even without an internet connection.

3

You listen

Rich audio plays immediately: a vivid description of the work, its history, its technique, its place in the artist's career. Pause, rewind, or move on at your own pace.

4

You explore

Spatial audio guides you to nearby works. Proximity triggers let you know when you're close to something worth stopping for. The museum becomes navigable by sound.

The technology, demystified

Image recognition

Gestalt runs a vision model called CLIP, trained to understand images the way humans do — by meaning, not just pixels. When you point at a Monet, the model doesn't look for "Monet" in the corner. It recognizes the visual signature of the work itself.

No QR codes

QR codes fail for accessibility. They require precise framing, steady hands, and visual acuity. Gestalt works with a glance — no scanning, no alignment, no "move closer" prompts. The camera does the work, not the visitor.

AI-generated descriptions

Writing audio descriptions for thousands of artworks would take years. Gestalt uses large language models to generate vivid, accurate descriptions in seconds — then surfaces them to curators for review. Human quality at machine scale.

Neural text-to-speech

Our voices don't sound robotic because they aren't. Modern TTS models produce natural inflection, pacing, and emphasis — voices you'd want to listen to for an hour-long gallery visit.

The invisible innovation

The best technology disappears. You don't think about how Gestalt works — you just point your phone and suddenly you understand what you're looking at. That simplicity is the innovation. Everything complex happens before the visitor ever opens the app.

See it in action

Try Gestalt at the Philadelphia Museum of Art — 302 works, fully indexed, free to experience.
